Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 7, 2026

1. Overview

Bullion Master is designed with a local-first approach. Most business records are stored on your device, and cloud features are used only for specific account, backup, restore, sync, and subscription workflows. This Privacy Policy explains what information may be processed when you use the app, how it is used, and what control remains with you.

2. Data Stored Locally

Depending on features you use, local storage may include:

  • Ledger/business records: parties, items, transactions, expenses, income, stock/opening balances, reports context.
  • Staff and payroll data: staff profile details and salary payment history.
  • App settings and security preferences: units, categories, PIN protection settings, device-linked preferences, and backup configuration references.
  • Attachments added by you (for example bill/record images) through app attachment workflows.
  • Operational state generated by the app, such as archive reports, startup progress metadata, opening stock context, and other internal app records required for feature continuity.

3. Account and Authentication Data

To use authenticated features, account data such as email and auth identifiers are processed via authentication services. This supports sign in, verification, profile access, device registration, subscription entitlement checks, and account recovery flows.

Depending on the feature, we may also process limited device or installation identifiers to help with account security, device management, session integrity, or feature enforcement.

4. Cloud Backup and Sync Data

If you enable cloud backup/sync features, your backup package and backup metadata (for example backup identifier/timestamp) are used to support restore and multi-device continuity.

  • Backups may be handled in encrypted format based on your configured backup/password workflow.
  • Access to backup contents depends on valid credentials/password where encryption is enabled.
  • Cloud features require active network connectivity and supported account state.
  • Backup content can include the business records and configuration data you choose to store in the app, subject to the backup flow you use.

5. Security and Integrity Events

The app may process local security events to protect data access, such as PIN checks, failed-attempt counters, auto-wipe configuration, and emergency/duress-triggered protection flows.

These protections are intended to reduce unauthorized access risk, but no device, software, storage, or transmission method can be guaranteed fully secure in every case.

6. How We Use Information

  • To operate core ledger, inventory, reporting, archive, and workflow features.
  • To authenticate users and maintain account access.
  • To provide backup, restore, sync, export, and sharing flows requested by you.
  • To protect access through security checks, PIN workflows, and device-related safeguards.
  • To validate subscription, trial, and feature entitlement state.

7. Permissions We Use

  • Internet/Network: required for login/auth, cloud backup/restore, and subscription checks.
  • Camera/Photos: used only when you capture or select images for record attachments.
  • Filesystem/Cache/Share: used for exports, local backup files, open file actions, and user-initiated sharing.

8. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your business data. Data may be shared only as required for service operation (for example authentication/cloud infrastructure) or when you explicitly trigger actions like export/share/restore.

We may also disclose information where required by applicable law, lawful process, or to protect rights, security, fraud prevention, or service integrity.

9. Retention and Deletion

  • Local app data stays on your device until you edit, delete, wipe, or reinstall/reset app data.
  • Restore operations can overwrite current local records with backup contents.
  • Security wipe flows may permanently remove local records.
  • Cloud backup retention may depend on the storage service, account state, overwrite actions, or deletion actions initiated by you.

10. Your Controls and Responsibilities

You can manage your information using built-in tools such as edit screens, export/backup, restore, and account/security settings. Because backup security depends on your credentials, keep your PIN and backup password confidential.

You are responsible for verifying what information you enter, choosing whether to use cloud or attachment features, maintaining secure access to your device and account, and testing your own backup and recovery process.

11. Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect feature, legal, or operational changes. The latest version and date shown in the app are the governing version.
