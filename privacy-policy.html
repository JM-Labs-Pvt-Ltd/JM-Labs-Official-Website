Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 7, 2026

1. Overview

Bullion Master is designed with a local-first approach. Most business records are stored on your device, and cloud features are used only for specific account, backup, restore, sync, and subscription workflows. This Privacy Policy explains what information may be processed when you use the app, how it is used, and what control remains with you.

2. Data stored locally

  • Ledger and business records such as parties, items, transactions, expenses, income, stock, and reports context.
  • Staff details and salary-related history where those workflows are used.
  • App settings, security preferences, device-linked preferences, and backup references.
  • Attachments added by you, such as bill or record images.
  • Operational metadata needed to keep app features working properly.

3. Account and authentication data

To use authenticated features, account data such as email and authentication identifiers may be processed. This supports sign-in, verification, profile access, device registration, subscription entitlement checks, and account recovery flows.

Depending on the feature, limited device or installation identifiers may also be processed to support account security, device management, session integrity, or feature enforcement.

4. Cloud backup and sync data

If you enable cloud backup or sync features, backup packages and related metadata may be used to support restore and multi-device continuity.

  • Backups may be handled in encrypted format depending on your workflow.
  • Access to backup contents may depend on valid credentials or backup passwords.
  • Cloud features require network connectivity and a supported account state.
  • Backup content can include the business records and configuration data you choose to store.

5. Security and integrity events

The app may process local security events to protect access, such as PIN checks, failed-attempt counters, auto-wipe configuration, and emergency protection flows.

These controls are intended to reduce unauthorized access risk, but no device, software, storage, or transmission method can be guaranteed fully secure in every case.

6. How information is used

  • To operate ledger, inventory, reporting, archive, and workflow features.
  • To authenticate users and maintain account access.
  • To provide backup, restore, sync, export, and sharing flows requested by you.
  • To protect access through security checks, PIN workflows, and device safeguards.
  • To validate subscription, trial, and feature entitlement state.

7. Permissions used

  • Internet or network access for login, backup, restore, and subscription checks.
  • Camera or photos access only when you capture or select images for records.
  • Filesystem, cache, or share access for exports, local backup files, and user-initiated sharing.

8. Data sharing and disclosure

Business data is not sold. Information may be shared only as needed for service operation, such as authentication or cloud infrastructure, or when you explicitly trigger export, share, restore, or similar actions.

Information may also be disclosed where required by applicable law, lawful process, fraud prevention, security protection, or service integrity needs.

9. Retention and deletion

  • Local app data remains on your device until you edit, delete, wipe, or reset it.
  • Restore operations can overwrite current local records with backup contents.
  • Security wipe flows may permanently remove local records.
  • Cloud backup retention may depend on service state, overwrite actions, or deletion actions initiated by you.

10. Your controls and responsibilities

You can manage information using built-in tools such as edit screens, export and backup workflows, restore options, and account or security settings. Because backup security depends on your credentials, keep your PIN and backup password confidential.

You are responsible for verifying the information you enter, deciding whether to use cloud or attachment features, maintaining secure access to your device and account, and testing your own backup and recovery process.

11. Policy updates

This Privacy Policy may be updated to reflect feature, legal, or operational changes. The latest published version and date are the governing version.
